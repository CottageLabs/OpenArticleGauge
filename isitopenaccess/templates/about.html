About the OpenArticleGauge and HowOpenIsIt

The OpenArticleGauge service is part of HowOpenIsIt, a suite of tools developed by PLOS, SPARC, and OASPA to make it easier to determine how open journals and journal articles are. The initial development of OAG was funded by PLOS and carried out by a team from CottageLabs [link].

The OpenArticleGauge provides information on how open a specific journal article is. It does this by going to the article itself and looking for licensing information. The service is available as a website, an API, and as source code. For more details on the software architecture and design see the developers section.

License information for each article is determined by following the DOI or Pubmed link to the article page and obtaining license information from the page itself. Each publisher site has an individual plugin to deal with the specifics of how license information is available.

By preference plugins search for the full text of the license statement, and check this against a known list of license statements for that publisher. Where possible license terms are obtained from a specified tag within an XML record. The response will tell provide both the means by which the license was determined (page scraping, an XML record) and the precise text that was obtained from the page.
